### {{N}}.2 Legal basis

We rely on the following lawful bases under GDPR Art. 6 for AI processing:

- **Contractual necessity (Art. 6(1)(b))** — where the AI feature is part of the product you signed up for, for example {{FEATURE_X}}.
- **Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f))** — where we use AI to secure the service, prevent abuse, or improve product quality. You can object under Art. 21.
- **Consent (Art. 6(1)(a))** — where we ask you directly, for example before sending your content to a generative AI feature.

We do not use AI to make any decision based solely on automated processing that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you, within the meaning of GDPR Art. 22(1). Any decision that could have such an effect is reviewed and taken by a human, with you having the right to contest it.

### {{N}}.5 Retention

Prompts and outputs sent to AI processors are retained by {{COMPANY_NAME}} for no longer than {{RETENTION_PERIOD, e.g. 30 days}} and are deleted automatically thereafter. Vendor retention is governed by the applicable DPA — typically zero-retention for the inference call itself.

### {{N}}.6 Your rights — how to exercise them

You can:

- **Request access** to personal data processed by any AI system, including meaningful information about the logic (Art. 15(1)(h));
- **Object** to AI processing based on legitimate interest (Art. 21);
- **Request human review** of any decision with legal or similarly significant effects (Art. 22(3));
- **Withdraw consent** where consent was the basis (Art. 7(3));
- **Request rectification** of inaccurate outputs stored about you (Art. 16);
- **Request erasure** (Art. 17).

We respond within one month. To exercise any right, email **{{PRIVACY_EMAIL}}**.
